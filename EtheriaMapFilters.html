HISTORY OF ETHERIA

Etheria is the first decentralized virtual world and limited-supply collectible launched on the Ethereum blockchain. It was created by Cyrus Adkisson, an early founding investor in the Ethereum ICO. Its deployment marked several significant milestones in blockchain history very early on, such as onchain generativeness, onchain marketplaces using offering systems, onchain physics, onchain assets with several unique traits, onchain sculpting of uniquely shaped blocks, and more. The first functional version, v0.9, was deployed on October 19, 2015. In total, four unique versions were deployed: v0.9 (Oct 19), v1.0 (Oct 22), v1.1 (Oct 29), and v1.2 (Nov 1). Each version is visually similar but technically distinct, carrying its own specific history and on-chain activity. Notably, Etheria predates the ERC-721 NFT standard, the ERC-20 token standard, and the very concept of "NFTs" as they are known today. It established its presence long before other popular projects such as CryptoPunks, MoonCats, CryptoKitties, Decentraland, Autoglyphs, Doodles, Bored Ape Yacht Club, and Pudgy Penguins.


The project consists of a hexagonal grid of 1,089 tiles, each representing a piece of interactive digital land; 457 of these are ownable, while 632 are not. At the time of deployment, the cost to purchase a tile was 1 ETH - approximately one dollar at the time. Due to the nascent market and a general lack of Ethereum awareness in 2015, only a few dozen tiles were sold across all versions, and an even smaller number of users actively farmed or edited blocks on their tiles. A defining moment occurred on November 13, 2015, when Cyrus presented Etheria at Devcon1 in London to a core group of Ethereum’s earliest developers and investors. To this day, the project contains historic comments from influential figures such as Vitalik Buterin, Taylor Gerring, Avsa, Joseph Lubin, Ken Erwin, Nick Johnson, and official Ethereum accounts. Following this initial period, the digital world remained largely dormant for years.


In early 2021, spurred by the rediscovery of early blockchain projects by "NFT archaeologists," interest in Etheria surged. This led to the rapid claiming of all remaining tiles that had sat unpurchased since 2015. Today, Etheria stands as the monument to early blockchain experimentation. With its provenance and immutable history verified directly on the Ethereum mainnet, its cultural significance is a permanent fixture of blockchain heritage.


The map you are currently viewing utilizes live on-chain data to visualize the unique traits of each tile, including elevation, coastal adjacency, location, and spatial relationships. Advanced metrics, such as the Tile Height Score (THS), allow for the comparison of tile statistics like never before. function getUint8FromByte32(bytes32 _b32, uint8 byteindex) public constant returns(uint8) {

uint numdigits = 64;

uint upperpowervar = 16 ** (numdigits - (byteindex*2));

uint lowerpowervar = 16 ** (numdigits - 2 - (byteindex*2));

uint postheadchop;

if(byteindex == 0)

postheadchop = buint;

else

postheadchop = buint % upperpowervar;

uint remainder = postheadchop % lowerpowervar;

uint evenedout = postheadchop - remainder;

uint b = evenedout / lowerpowervar;

return uint8(b);

}
